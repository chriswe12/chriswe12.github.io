Dual-Arm Assembly Grasp Planning

KUKA iiwa 7 · Assembly · Constraint filtering · RL control

My master thesis focuses on task-aware grasp planning and execution for autonomous dual-arm assembly on two KUKA iiwa 7 robots. The central problem is that a grasp can be geometrically valid but still useless for assembly if it blocks the motion, violates constraints, or prevents the second arm from completing the task.

The pipeline generates part-specific grasps offline, filters them for assembly feasibility through constraint satisfaction, and selects feasible grasps online from estimated object poses. The execution side connects the selected grasp to an RL-based real-robot controller on the KUKA iiwa 7 setup.

The work combines manipulation planning, feasibility filtering, pose-driven online selection, and real-robot execution. The emphasis is on making grasp choice depend on the downstream assembly task rather than treating grasping as an isolated problem.
