Two-Stage Coaxial Transmission Design

Creo · GD&T · DIN standards · bearings and seals · manufacturing drawings

In a three-person design project at TU Vienna, we designed a two-stage coaxial spur gearbox from specification to documented CAD package. The input was a 60 kW, 2800 rpm drive with a 300 rpm output target, defined service factors, bearing-life requirements, and a cast housing constraint.

My work covered standards-based component sizing, CAD design in Creo, technical drawings, and supplier-part selection. The design package included shaft and gear dimensioning, bearing and seal selection, assembly drawings, workshop drawings, GD&T, and calculation documentation aligned with the course's DIN-based requirements.

The value of the project was the full mechanical design loop: translating a powertrain specification into manufacturable geometry, checking components against norms, and producing drawings detailed enough for review and fabrication planning.
